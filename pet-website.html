Your How To Guide to Living in Harmony with Opposing Species

Meet Your Guides:

Hello! I am Sam's dog Mafalda.

I am 15 years old and still cute! I've been living around cats for years and I have some advice for living with our feline friends

  1. Do not try to eat the cat. They may seem delicious, but I promise it won't end well.
  2. Lick the cat at your own risk. Some of them like it, some of them tolerate it and some of them will scratch your eye out.
  3. Cats hate the following things:
    • Water
    • Vacuums (we have this in common!)
    • Being bitten

And I am Aaron's cat Daisy!

I have never lived with a dog and I am a bit skeptical about them. Here are some of my primary concerns about dogs:

  • I hear they smell. Especially when they get that gross water stuff on them.
  • They make a lot of noise, which would be distressing to my sensitive ears
  • They seem so needy what with always demanding attention from the humans

How to coexist

I assume that the most important part of coexistence with the enemy would be having adequate space for personal time. I like to enjoy sitting by myself on the couch and not being disturbed. I think this would be a great time for the human to take the dog out somewhere I don't have to see or smell it!

The best advice I can give to any dog is to leave the cat alone. They can't even sit on command! We know we are smarter and superior. Do not let them trick you!

Cuddling

I cannot express strongly enough that no-one should ever try to snuggle a cat. When I am ready I will come and purr at you and walk onto the area I wish to sleep on.

Us puppies love to cuddle. We livefor cuddling. So if you're a cat, you can cuddle with us, but be careful. Some doggies like to play a little too much.
